What Is Siphoning?

Siphoning (also known as vacuum pumping or desludging) is the process of using specialized truck-mounted vacuum equipment to safely remove liquid and semi-solid waste from septic tanks, grease traps, cesspools, and other sewage systems.

A properly functioning septic tank is essential for a healthy and hygienic living or working environment. Over time, solids accumulate in the tank and must be periodically removed to maintain system performance and prevent overflow, backups, and costly damage.

At Joel Gacho, our certified siphoning specialists operate modern vacuum trucks and follow strict environmental protocols to ensure safe, responsible waste removal and disposal.

Signs Your System Needs Siphoning

😸

Foul Odors

Persistent sewage smells in or around your property, especially near the tank area, indicate it needs to be emptied.

🕶

Slow Drains

Multiple slow-draining sinks, toilets, or showers throughout the property suggest a full or nearly full septic tank.

🔴

Sewage Backups

Wastewater backing up into fixtures is a serious sign that your tank is at or over capacity and requires immediate service.

🌿

Lush Patch of Grass

Unusually green or fast-growing grass over the septic tank area often indicates leakage from an overfull tank.

📅

It's Been 1—3 Years

Even without visible symptoms, septic tanks should be pumped every 1—3 years depending on size and usage.

🔊

Gurgling Sounds

Gurgling or bubbling noises in pipes and drains after flushing point to a system under pressure from a full tank.
