Fast, Effective Clog Removal

A clogged drain or toilet is one of the most common and frustrating plumbing problems. While minor clogs can sometimes be cleared with a plunger, persistent or deep-seated blockages require professional intervention.

Our declogging specialists use professional-grade equipment — including high-pressure water jetting and mechanical augers — to clear even the most stubborn blockages quickly and completely, without damaging your pipes.

Common Causes of Clogs We Fix

🢐

Grease & Fat Buildup

Cooking oils and fats solidify in kitchen drain pipes over time, gradually restricting and eventually blocking flow.

🔗

Hair & Soap Scum

Hair and soap residue combine in bathroom drains to form tough, binding clogs that worsen over time.

🧹

Flushed Foreign Objects

Non-flushable items — wet wipes, cotton, hygiene products — easily lodge in pipes and cause major blockages.

🌿

Root Intrusion

Tree and plant roots can penetrate underground pipes, causing partial or complete blockages in main sewer lines.

💎

Mineral Scale

Hard water deposits (calcium, magnesium) build up inside pipes over years, narrowing the flow path until a blockage forms.

🚫

Collapsed or Offset Pipes

Old, damaged, or poorly installed pipes can collapse or shift, creating blockage points that require professional attention.

Our Declogging Process

1

Blockage Assessment

2

Method Selection

Depending on the blockage, we choose the most effective and pipe-safe method: manual snaking, high-pressure jetting, or a combination.

3

Clog Removal

Our technician clears the blockage completely — not just punching through it, but fully removing the obstructing material.

4

Flush & Flow Test

We run water through the cleared drain to verify full, unrestricted flow and confirm the blockage is completely gone.

5

Prevention Advice

We share practical tips to help prevent the same type of clog from recurring in your specific drain or system.

Preventing Future Clogs

The best way to deal with clogs is to prevent them. Here are our top prevention tips:

  • ✓ Install drain screens or strainers in all sinks and showers
  • ✓ Never pour grease, oil, or fat down the drain
  • ✓ Flush only human waste and toilet paper
  • ✓ Regularly flush drains with hot water to dissolve grease
  • ✓ Schedule annual professional drain cleaning
  • ✓ Avoid planting trees directly over sewer lines
